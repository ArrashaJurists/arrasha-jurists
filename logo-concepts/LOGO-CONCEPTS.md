# Arrasha Jurists LLP — Logo Concepts

## Design Principles (derived from the entire project)

Every element of this brand says the same thing: **restraint is the luxury signal.** The website uses dark backgrounds, gold accents, Cormorant Garamond serif, sharp edges, no emojis, no stock photos, no rounded corners. The logo must speak the same language.

**What premium Indian law firm logos do:**
- AZB Partners: pure wordmark, no symbol
- Khaitan & Co: pure wordmark, red accent
- Trilegal: clean wordmark with subtle mark
- S&R Associates: monogram + wordmark
- Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas: wordmark with red line

**What this logo must NOT do:**
- No scales of justice (cliche, every small firm uses them)
- No gavels, pillars, or legal iconography
- No gradients or 3D effects
- No rounded shapes (the entire design system uses sharp angles)
- No busy complexity — it must work at 24px favicon size and 200px header size

## Concept 1: The Architectural A
**File:** `concept-1-monogram.svg`

A serifed "A" constructed from pure lines — architectural, like a building elevation or a structural drawing. The crossbar sits slightly below center (classical proportion). Serif feet and an apex serif give it Cormorant Garamond's character without being a typeface glyph.

**Surrounding elements:**
- Corner accent marks (top-left, bottom-right) — drawn from the BCI modal's decorative corners. These become a brand motif: "we frame things precisely."
- A faint rotated diamond behind the A — drawn from the hero section's background element. Barely visible, adds depth.

**Why this works:**
- The "A" for Arrasha is immediately recognizable
- The architectural construction signals precision and structure — which is what the firm delivers (structured filings, organized evidence, methodical work)
- At small sizes (favicon), just the A in the diamond works
- At large sizes (letterhead), the full composition with corner marks works
- The line-drawn approach matches the website's typography-first, no-photos aesthetic

**Usage:**
- Favicon: just the A inside the diamond, 32x32
- Nav: A-diamond mark + "ARRASHA JURISTS LLP" wordmark
- Letterhead: full composition with corner accents
- Business card: A-diamond mark on front, wordmark on back

## Concept 2: Abstract Scales (NOT recommended)
**File:** `concept-2-scales.svg`

Included for completeness — scales of justice reduced to pure geometric lines. The triangular pans, central pillar, and balance beam are all single-weight strokes.

**Why this is here but not recommended:**
- Even abstracted, scales are the most overused symbol in legal branding
- RegisterKaro, Vakilsearch, and hundreds of small firms use scales
- Premium firms (AZB, Khaitan, Trilegal) never use legal iconography
- It contradicts the "not a platform, not a vendor" positioning

**If you still want it:** It could work as a watermark or background pattern element, not as the primary logo.

## Concept 3: Pure Wordmark
**File:** `concept-3-wordmark.svg`

"Arrasha Jurists" in Cormorant Garamond with a vertical gold accent line to the left. "Jurists" in gold. A thin underline runs the width. "LLP" below left, "STRATEGIC LEGAL COUNSEL" tagline below right.

**Why this works:**
- This is what the most prestigious firms do — the name IS the brand
- The vertical gold line is the only non-typographic element, and it's a direct echo of the gold dividers used throughout the website
- Works perfectly in the nav bar (already close to what the site uses)
- Scales beautifully from business card to building signage

**The vertical line is the key differentiator.** It turns a wordmark into a mark. It says: "we draw the line" — between professional and careless, between scoped and vague, between transparent and opaque. This is the firm that draws clear lines for its clients.

**Usage:**
- Nav: horizontal layout as shown
- Letterhead: horizontal, top-left
- Business card: horizontal, centered
- Favicon: just the "A" with the vertical line to its left

## Concept 4: Diamond AJ Monogram (RECOMMENDED)
**File:** `concept-4-diamond-aj.svg`

"AJ" inside a double diamond (outer solid, inner faint). The A is cream, the J is gold — matching the "Arrasha Jurists" brand text treatment. Below: the stacked wordmark.

**Why this is the strongest concept:**

1. **It's already part of the design language.** The rotated diamond appears in the hero section, the corner accents appear on the BCI modal, the gold square markers appear on the growth journey. This logo doesn't introduce a new element — it crystallizes existing ones.

2. **"AJ" is distinctive.** Unlike a single "A" (which could be any firm), "AJ" is unambiguously Arrasha Jurists. The cream A + gold J mirrors the website's name treatment.

3. **The diamond means something.** In Indian legal culture, precision and clarity are paramount. A diamond — sharp edges, symmetrical, no curves — is the geometric expression of the brief's design rules (no rounded corners, sharp edges everywhere). It also echoes the tilak/bindi motif in a completely abstracted way.

4. **It scales perfectly:**
   - 16px favicon: diamond outline + AJ, no wordmark needed
   - 32px social avatar: diamond + AJ, legible
   - Nav header: diamond mark + horizontal wordmark
   - Letterhead: diamond mark centered above wordmark
   - Stamp/seal: diamond works as a seal shape

5. **It's not a legal cliche.** No scales, no gavels, no pillars. It's a geometric identity that could belong to a luxury brand, an architecture firm, or a private bank — which is exactly the positioning tier the firm aspires to.

**How to refine this into a final logo:**
- Take this SVG to a designer (or Figma)
- Adjust the A and J letterspacing and positioning within the diamond
- Test at 16px, 32px, 64px, 200px — ensure legibility at all sizes
- Create dark-on-light and light-on-dark variants
- Create a horizontal lockup (diamond + wordmark side by side) for the nav
